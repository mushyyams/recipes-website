steps:
  - "Cook pasta in well-salted water until just shy of al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining."
  - "In a wide skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Swirl constantly until golden brown and nutty-smelling, 4–6 minutes."
  - "Add sage leaves — they'll sizzle and crisp. Cook 30 seconds."
  - "Add pasta, a splash of pasta water, lemon zest, and half the cheese. Toss vigorously until silky."
  - "Finish with lemon juice, remaining cheese, pepper, and flaky salt. Serve immediately."

Brown butter is a mood. It's also a technique that rewards patience and punishes distraction — walk away for thirty seconds and you're making black butter pasta, which is a different article entirely.

## On the pasta shape

Cavatappi catches the sauce in its curves. Linguine coats evenly. Use what you have; the butter does the heavy lifting.

## A note on cheese

Pre-grated parmesan contains anti-caking agents that make the sauce grainy. Grate it yourself. Your future self will thank you.
