  - "2 tbsp soy sauce"
  - "4 cloves garlic, finely minced"
  - "1 tbsp rice vinegar"
  - "1 tsp sesame oil"
  - "½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes"
  - "2 tbsp butter"
  - "Fresh scallions and sesame seeds, to finish"
steps:
  - "Whisk honey, soy sauce, garlic, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes in a small bowl."
  - "Season salmon with salt. Heat a large skillet over medium-high with a thin coat of oil."
  - "Sear salmon skin-side up for 3–4 minutes until golden. Flip and cook 2 minutes more."
  - "Reduce heat to medium. Add butter and pour in the glaze. Baste continuously for 2–3 minutes until sticky and lacquered."
  - "Rest 2 minutes. Top with scallions and sesame seeds. Serve with rice and something green."

I posted this recipe on a Tuesday night when I had fifteen minutes and two hungry roommates staring at me. The comment section asked for the glaze ratio about four hundred times, so here it is — written down properly, with the one detail that makes it work.

## Why this glaze sticks (literally)

Most honey garlic salmon recipes fail because the pan is too cool or the salmon is too wet. You need **high heat at the start** for the sear, then **medium heat** for the glaze. That temperature shift is everything.

> "The moment the glaze goes from shiny to thick — like maple syrup on a cold pancake — pull it off the heat."

## The TikTok timing trick

Film the basting. People love watching the glaze bubble and cling. But for actual eating: don't skip the two-minute rest. The salmon keeps cooking and the sauce sets into something almost candy-like.

## What to serve it with

Steamed jasmine rice is the obvious answer. I also love this over cold soba noodles with cucumber — unexpected, but the sweet-salty glaze loves something neutral and slurpable underneath.
